How the 6 pools are carved (in software)
One flat network: all 30 machines share a single subnet — simplest possible setup; every node is reachable by the Spark driver and the Kubernetes API.
Logical pools: Kubernetes namespaces + Spark fair-scheduler pools + node labels / selectors split the 30 nodes into 6 sub-clusters, each with its own CPU / GPU / memory quota — no VLANs needed.
Security caveat: logical pools isolate resources & scheduling, not the wire. Add K8s NetworkPolicies for pod-level firewalling — and keep the Cybersecurity lab on a separate VLAN / air-gap for risky work.
